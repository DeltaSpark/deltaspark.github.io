Patexia Inc.

Manager, Case Research
November 2013 to present

A dual-market facing startup in Santa Monica, Patexia’s mission is to open the way for innovation and improve patent quality through crowdsourcing. We accomplish that with a community of over 10,000 experts available for clients to harness through crowdsourced studies or direct consulting engagements.

My main job is to manage the crowdsourced studies by designing a clear and concise contest for the crowd to interface with and then distilling the results into actionable reports for our clients. My wider mandate is to design and implement the processes and technology that will help our business get to scale.

Mass Analysis of Patent Litigation

For their May/June 2015, IAM asked Patexia to explain how the America Invents Act has changed the landscape for patent owners.

To support the thesis made by Pedram Sameni, the author of the article and Patexia's CEO, I analyzed trends across 5,000 patent litigation cases to show how patent litigation defendants were increasingly aggressive in defending themselves against invalid patents.

Writing a NASA Proposal

At the start of 2015, NASA reached out to various crowdsourcing companies for proposals on how crowdsourcing could help the government agency solicit new, innovative ideas.

As part of Patexia's proposal, I wrote the technical and past performance sections, explaining how Patexia could leverage our expertise in running crowdsourced studies for NASA.

In June 2015, Patexia was awarded the contract, alongside 9 other companies that have helped pioneer crowdsourcing in the US.
