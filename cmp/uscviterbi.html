the Kinematics of Reaching project

As a master's student, I worked at SangerLab with Dr. Matteo Bertucco and Professor Terry Sanger studying the kinematics of reaching in children with motor-disabilities while they were interacting with touchscreens.

The Matlab algorithms we designed allowed us to analyize motion capture and electromyography data, and make recommendations on the UI adjustments necessary to make it easier for these patients to interact with our digital world.

Investigation of Kinematic and Muscle Pattern of Upper Arm Movements

Children with movement disorders are often characterized by unwanted postures and suboptimal execution during goal-directed tasks, in which jerky and imprecise movements are extrinsic performance features.

the Human Remote project

For my senior capstone project, my group and I created a gesture-based user interface using the Xbox 360 Kinect and the LabVIEW development platform. Using our "Human Remote," a user could send 9 different commands by tracing paths in the air with their hands.

Our project won first prize out of 14 teams at the Viterbi Senior Design Expo.
